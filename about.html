Background:

I took Intro Statistics in the spring of 2018, and it was the first class in my life that I actually enjoyed.

I started "learning to code" in the fall of 2018 and, like most people just starting out, I was very bad at it. Of course, in the era of Copilot, ChatGPT, and similar GenAI tools, this process is becoming even easier. My personal approach involves spending a significant amount of time planning and researching project requirements, and then using these tools to help me implement boilerplate code before going in and making the specific modifications.

In my free time, I enjoy solving programming problems in DSA, computational mathematics, and data science. I personally recommend Neetcode, Project Euler, and StrataScratch (respectively) for these purposes.
